As of the report date, these combined fees and shares amount to an aggregate of 6% of the gross Staking Consideration, with the Trust retaining the remainder.
  • The Trust's Ether will be staked directly from its wallets administered by the Custodian, and the Trust retains control, including the ability to unstake its Ether. The Trust's Ether is not commingled with other Ether holders during staking.
  • The Sponsor intends to stake all of the Trust's Ether at all times, with exceptions for paying fees, expenses, satisfying redemption requests, distributing Staking Consideration, or if staking raises regulatory concerns or vulnerabilities.
  • A "Liquidity Sleeve" of unstaked Ether will be maintained to manage liquidity for redemption requests, with the percentage being dynamic and adjusted based on market activity. The approximate percentage of staked Ether will be reported daily on etfs.grayscale.com/eth.
  • The Trust is permitted to engage in staking only if the "Staking Condition" is satisfied, meaning it should not cause the Trust to be treated as other than a grantor trust for U.S. federal income tax purposes, supported by a tax opinion or ruling.
  • Shareholders may incur tax liabilities from Staking Consideration without receiving corresponding distributions, as the IRS considers staking rewards as current, ordinary income for U.S. federal income tax purposes.
  • The Trust's Ether is subject to "Slashing," a blockchain network penalty that can permanently reduce or confiscate staked Digital Assets or rewards due to validator misbehavior. Coinbase's responsibility in such events is limited, and compensation may not cover all losses.
  • Coinbase explicitly disclaims warranties for its staking services, which are provided "as is" and "as available," and does not guarantee any rewards from staking.
  • The Trust's Ether is held in the United States, and any change in location requires written client approval, except in security or disaster recovery events.
  • Coinbase Custody is a fiduciary under New York Banking Law and a qualified custodian, holding the Trust's Digital Assets as fiduciary assets, not commingled with its own or other clients' assets.
  • The Prime Broker Agreement includes provisions for dispute resolution through arbitration in New York and outlines limitations of liability for Coinbase, generally not exceeding the greater of fees paid in the last 12 months or the value of the Digital Assets involved, with specific caps for cold storage addresses ($100,000,000 USD).
