US equity markets demonstrated mixed performance in early October, with the S&P 500 retreating 0.42% to 6,712.27 despite maintaining strong YTD gains of 14.12%. The index reached an all-time high of 6,754.49 during the session before pulling back on concerns over Oracle's chip leasing challenges and Tesla's Model Y unveiling event 1. Political uncertainty from the ongoing federal government shutdown, now in its seventh consecutive day, contributed to investor unease alongside fresh NY Fed data showing consumers expect higher inflation one and five years ahead.

S&P 500 Performance and Sector Dynamics

The S&P 500 posted solid quarterly gains through September, advancing 8.1% in Q3 with a 3.6% September return on a total return basis 8. Technology and Communication Services led sector performance in Q3, gaining 13.2% and 12.0% respectively, while Consumer Discretionary added 9.5%. Consumer Staples was the only sector posting negative returns at -2.4% 8. Recent sector rotation favored Materials (+0.9%), Consumer Discretionary (+0.8%), and Health Care (+0.7%), while Consumer Staples lagged at -0.1% 12.

Value factors significantly outperformed in August, driven by a steepening yield curve and increased Fed rate cut expectations. The best performing factors were E/P (5.35% return), B/P (5.05% return), and D/Y (1.81% return), while ROE and Price Momentum posted negative returns of 1.46% and 4.57% respectively 26. Small caps experienced their best month versus large caps since July 2024, with the Russell 2000 gaining 7.14% on a total return basis, outperforming the Russell MidCap (+2.50%) and Russell 1000 (+2.10%) 28.

NASDAQ Technology Leadership

The NASDAQ closed at 24,841.47, down 0.55% but maintaining robust YTD performance of 18.22% and one-year gains of 25.46%. The index showed strong September performance with a 5.68% total return, contributing to an 11.42% Q3 gain 8. Technology stocks led the charge, driven by AI-related gains in semiconductors and strong performances from software and hardware companies 15. The Bloomberg Magnificent Seven Index gained 0.7% recently, indicating profit-taking occurred in smaller companies while mega-cap tech maintained momentum 21.

AI investment continues to drive performance, supported by developments such as the $1 trillion Saudi Public Investment Fund actively seeking more AI bets. The AI thematic has broadened beyond US megacaps, with AI-linked stocks in other regions keeping pace or outperforming key US peers 7. Nearly 140 basis points of rate cuts are priced into swap markets through next September, suggesting significant financial easing to support continued rallies 21.

FTSE 100 Resilience and Outperformance

The FTSE 100 demonstrated remarkable resilience, closing at 9,483.58 with a modest 0.05% gain and YTD performance of 16.04%. The index has been one of Europe's strongest performers, posting a 14% rally YTD and outperforming broader European equities by approximately 5% 10. Healthcare stocks drove recent performance, with AstraZeneca, Hikma Pharmaceuticals, GSK, and Pfizer contributing significantly 3. The index benefits from limited exposure to AI and chip stocks, with defensive sectors leading gains including Consumer Staples (+2.34%), Health Care (+2.11%), and Utilities (+1.94%) 32.

The FTSE 100's outperformance stems from several factors: UK companies have less exposure to industrials and tech sectors affected by tariffs, generally lower valuations make them appealing alternatives to US investments, and the inverse relationship between sterling and local stocks has re-established 36. The index remains the most cheaply valued among EU indices despite its YTD performance, with implied volatility at compelling levels of 10.2% for 3-month ATMF options 10.
