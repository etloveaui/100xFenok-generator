The 10-year Treasury yield closed at 4.21%, down 0.73% for the session and marking its third consecutive daily decline to reach the lowest closing level since briefly dipping below 4.2% earlier this month.48 The yield traded as low as 4.2014%, just above August's previous low of 4.1826%.4 Treasuries experienced a bull steepener with the 2-10 year spread widening to approximately 60 basis points, nearing its steepest level since January 2022.627

Performance and Curve Dynamics

The Treasury curve saw mixed performance with front-end underperformance and long-end strength. The 2-year yield cheapened 2 bps to 3.629%, while the 10-year richened 3.1 bps to 4.205% and the 30-year fell 4.8 bps to 4.874%.6 This reversed recent curve steepening trades, with the 2-10 spread tightening to 57 bps from 62 bps.6 The 5-year and 30-year spread reached 116 bps, the largest since 2021, indicating heightened investor concern about yield curve steepening.5

Key Market Drivers

The bond rally was supported by political developments surrounding Federal Reserve independence, specifically the likely fast-tracking of Stephen Miran's nomination to the Fed's Board of Governors, as he is seen as advocating for deeper rate cuts.4 President Trump's dismissal of Fed Governor Lisa Cook contributed to expectations of lower rates in the short term, with markets pricing in an 87.2% chance of a quarter-point cut in September.8 Flight-to-quality buying also supported bonds due to geopolitical concerns.4

Economic data showed mixed impacts on Treasury performance. The US economy expanded at a revised 3.3% annualized pace in Q2, up from the initially reported 3.0%, with business investment expanding at 5.7% versus the initially reported 1.9%.23 However, bond traders largely shrugged off the upbeat GDP data.8 Initial jobless claims fell to 229,000 for the week ended August 23, slightly above estimates of 227,500, while continuing claims dropped to 1.95M from 1.96M.12

Auction Results and Supply Outlook

The $44B 7-year note auction cleared at 3.925%, tailing 0.3 bps versus the bid deadline rate of 3.922%.7 The stop rate of 3.925% marked the lowest since September 2024, down 16.7 bps from July's 4.092%.7 Demand was mixed with a 2.49 cover ratio, weaker than the 2.64 average and the weakest since May 2024.7 Indirect bidders took a hefty 77.4%, better than the 69.5% average and the best since May 2022.7

Treasury bill auctions showed mixed results with the $100B 4-week bill clearing at 4.245%, 5.5 bps richer than the previous week, while the $85B 8-week bill stopped at 4.145%, 7.5 bps below last week.14 No Treasury supply is expected next week, with $58B in 3-year notes, $39B in 10-year reopenings, and $22B in 30-year reopenings scheduled for the week of September 8th.19

Analyst Commentary and Outlook

Asset allocators remain underweight US Treasuries, with managers having marginally trimmed their underweight positions closer to neutral.9 Foreign central banks now hold more gold than US Treasuries for the first time since 1996.1 IMF Deputy Managing Director Gita Gopinath warned that bond markets are in a "fragile place" due to incredibly high debt levels, pointing to signs of concern in French, UK, and US long-yield markets.21

Real yields are expected to rise despite potential government interference in monetary policy, with a leading indicator suggesting US 10-year real yields should increase over the next 3-4 months.25 The Bloomberg TIPS index shows mean-reverting behavior with TIPS currently overbought, suggesting potential falls that would lead to rising real yields.25 Markets are pricing in some risk that increased politicization of the central bank could lead to inappropriately low rates short-term and higher inflation and rates long-term.12
